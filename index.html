Overview

Data usability plagues the healthcare industry. Clinical records are simultaneously the most sensitive data and the key to unlocking the next medical breakthroughs. Our scientific areas of focus include privacy, trust in ML and efficiency. For privacy, we investigate ways to generate data with high predictive value that is differentially private. For engendering trust in ML, we investigate ways to explain ML models to a physician. For efficiency, we investigate ways to improve the speed of ML algorithms, e.g., finding nearest neighbors in clinical embeddings. While the team’s north star is healthcare, the challenges and solutions apply to data from any domain.

Projects

  • Private Synthetic Data -- Can we use LLMs to generate patient records that are private yet have ML predictive value?
  • Fact-Check the Output of LLMs -- Can we help a physician quickly verify a claim such as ‘patient took blood thinners’ from a 200-page clinical record using LLMs?
  • Nearest Neighbor Search -- Can we better understand embeddings and speed up nearest neighbor search to support semantic search and RAG use cases in clinical contexts?
  • Summarizing Medical Jargon with Colloquial Text -- Can we translate a radiology report written in medical jargon to a colloquial, patient-friendly version using LLMs?
